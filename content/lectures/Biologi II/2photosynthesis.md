# boogus

Photosynthesis is the process plants use to turn **sunlight**, **water**, and **carbon dioxide** into food (sugar) and oxygen. It happens mostly in the leaves.

## The Simple Version

Plants are like tiny solar-powered kitchens. They take in light and raw ingredients, then cook up their own energy.

- **Light** comes from the sun
- **Water** comes up from the roots
- **Carbon dioxide** comes in from the air
- **Oxygen** is released as a bonus for us to breathe

## The Word Equation

Carbon dioxide + Water + Light energy → Glucose + Oxygen

## Where It Happens

| Part of the plant | What it does |
| ----------------- | ------------------------------------- |
| Leaves            | Main site of photosynthesis           |
| Chloroplasts      | Tiny structures that capture light    |
| Chlorophyll       | The green pigment that absorbs light  |
| Roots             | Pull water up from the soil           |

## Why It Matters

1. It feeds the plant so it can grow.
2. It produces the oxygen that animals and people breathe.
3. It's the starting point for almost every food chain on Earth.

> **Key idea:** No sunlight, no photosynthesis — and without photosynthesis, there would be very little life on Earth.

## Quick Recap

- Plants make their own food using light.
- The green stuff (chlorophyll) does the light-catching.
- We get oxygen out of the deal.
